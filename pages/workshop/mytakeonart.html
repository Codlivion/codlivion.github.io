My Take On Art

Art, in my view, is a way of expression in various forms.

In literature, it is implemented with words, language and writing methods.

Drawing and painting are a visual presantation of worlds and objects on a flat view.

Music involves generating sounds in a particular way.

Sculpture and architecture would define different methods and principles to model, shape or build objects or structures.

The idea in acting and cinematics is to express through animation and simulation, seen from the viewer's eye.

And this is how I would summarize major arts.

It could be in the form of words, sights, sounds, creations, animations, a combination of these and maybe even some other, yet unknown or uninvented way.


I am, in one way or another, involved or interested in each of these art forms.

And I would in some way use each one in my works.

Making games, I need graphics so I draw, both physically for concept art, and digitally for actual game assets.

I would use synth softwares to generate audio sources from midi.

I'm interested in making games with a story, lore and dialogs so there goes a little bit of literature in it.

I may work with 3D games so I study and practice some 3D modelling from time to time.

And games would have cutscenes and various tecniques of screen presentation so we have some cinematics in it too.

Well, it may falsely seem like I'm claiming to know everything about art.

Where in reality I'm not an expert in any of them, neither am I a practitioner in each and every one of them.

Although I'm really interested in drawing and music, again I'm just as skilled as an individual, self learning, freestyle artist, intermediate at most.

But the point is, I'm interested in them and I appreciate and study their techniques regarding my own works.

I would be happy so long as I could draw whatever I visions I have, and compose whatever pieces that come from inside, and utilize art techniques to my works just the way I want it, in my own way.


There is a benefit, among others, in learning at least the basic principles of its related fields when you are occupied with some profession.

And that is to be able to define your field better, understand what it is and what it is not.

You can even draw parallels between them, see how they are connected, how they are different, and understand the virtues and shortcomings of each.

That is my general approach to arts.

Again I'm just a self-learning, self-claimed artist involved with drawing and composing, who appreciates any art technique that is creative or practical.

It is a way for me to express some aspects of my inner self.

So feel free to take a look at my works and samples, and see if any of my tricks could be any useful.
